title: 'Weathering the Waste: Inventory Choice, Market Aggregation and Food Loss Among Urban Street Vendors'
# abstract: 'The last ten years (2014–2023) have been the warmest decade on record globally (Lindsey & Dahlman, 2024), intensifying the vulnerability of informal food distribution systems in cities of the Global South. Street vendors, especially those selling perishable produce, face heightened risks of food loss and waste (FLW) due to climate exposure, inadequate infrastructure, and economic pressures. Practices such as distress selling and discarding unsold produce—reported frequently in focus groups and interviews—reduce vendor income and contribute to urban sanitation challenges. Despite the scale of these issues, FLW research remains concentrated in high-income countries and neglects the distribution stage in developing contexts, particularly among informal vendors. This project addresses these critical gaps by implementing a randomized controlled trial with 2,000 produce vendors (a subsample of a 3,000-vendor baseline) across 40 wards in Patna, India. The study evaluates three low-cost, scalable interventions: storage units, facilitation of bulk end-of-day sales, and training in sustainable waste disposal. By collecting repeated, high-frequency data on vendor productivity, food loss, weather exposure, and business practices, this research generates new empirical evidence on how simple adaptations can reduce FLW and enhance vendor livelihoods. In doing so, it reframes street vendors as key agents in climate adaptation and urban food system sustainability.'
